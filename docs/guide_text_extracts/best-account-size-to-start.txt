TITLE:
What Account Size Should I Start With?

SUBTITLE:
Choose the smallest size that comfortably fits your stop size, typical drawdown, and trade frequency—then scale on results.

MAIN CONTENT:
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Sizing checklist
 
 Typical stop size (ticks) × tick value
 Max simultaneous trades/contracts
 Daily loss limit cushion (≥ 2–3× avg risk)
 Trailing vs static drawdown behavior

STRUCTURED FAQ (Schema.org):
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Q: How do I match size to my stops?
A: Estimate your average stop (ticks × tick value) and max concurrent positions. Ensure daily limit and trailing DD can withstand expected variance.

Q: Start small or big?
A: Start small. Prove consistency, then scale. Oversizing early leads to rule breaches more than faster growth.
