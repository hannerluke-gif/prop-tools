What Are Futures?

  • Futures are financial contracts that obligate traders to buy or sell an asset at a set price on a future date.
  • Common futures: stock index futures (e.g., S&P 500), commodities (oil, gold), and currencies.

Why Trade Futures?

  1. Leverage: Control larger positions with less capital.
  2. Liquidity: Active markets allow quick entry and exit.
  3. Flexibility: Speculate on price going up or down.
  4. Risk Management: Futures can hedge other investments.

Common questions

What makes futures different from stocks?
Stocks represent ownership in a company, while futures are contracts tied to an underlying asset’s price.
Can I start trading futures with a small account?
Yes, micro contracts (e.g., MNQ, MES) allow beginners to trade futures with lower costs and smaller risk.
